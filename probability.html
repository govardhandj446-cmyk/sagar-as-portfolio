Probability Fundamentals

Probability measures how likely an event is to occur. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

Core Topics

  • Classical Probability is based on equally likely outcomes, such as dice or cards.
  • Empirical Probability uses observed frequencies from experiments.
  • Subjective Probability reflects belief or experience when exact data is unavailable.
  • Addition Rule finds the probability of either event occurring.
  • Multiplication Rule finds the probability of both events occurring.
  • Bayes’ Theorem calculates conditional probability using prior knowledge.

Examples

Dice and Cards

Probability of an even number on a die is 3/6 = 1/2; probability of drawing a king from a deck is 4/52 = 1/13.

Independent Events

For two independent events, P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B), such as coin toss and die roll.

Bayes’ Rule

Used to revise probability after observing new evidence, such as defective item source analysis.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain probability concepts, sample spaces, and event types.
  • Apply addition and multiplication rules correctly.
  • Interpret conditional probability and Bayes’ theorem.
  • Solve real-life probability problems.
  • Develop analytical and logical reasoning skills.
