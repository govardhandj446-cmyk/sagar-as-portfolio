Sampling and Inference

Statistical inference uses sample data to draw conclusions about a larger population. This section covers how to collect samples and test hypotheses.

Sampling Methods

Random Sampling

Every member of the population has an equal chance of being selected.

Stratified Sampling

Population divided into groups, with samples taken from each group to ensure representation.

Cluster Sampling

Population split into clusters and entire clusters are sampled.

Systematic Sampling

Select every k-th member from a list.

Hypothesis Testing

  • Null Hypothesis (H0) is the default assumption, such as no difference or no effect.
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H1) is what we test for, such as a difference or effect.
  • Significance Level (a) is the risk threshold for rejecting H0, commonly 0.05.
  • Type I Error occurs when a true null hypothesis is rejected.
  • Type II Error occurs when a false null hypothesis is not rejected.

Confidence Intervals & Sample Size

Confidence intervals provide a range for a population parameter. Sample size depends on confidence level, variability, and margin of error.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain sampling methods and their use in practice.
  • Distinguish between population and sample statistics.
  • Construct confidence intervals for population parameters.
  • Perform hypothesis tests using p-values and critical values.
  • Understand the trade-offs between sample size, confidence, and precision.
